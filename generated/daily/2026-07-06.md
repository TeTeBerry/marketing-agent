**Topic:** Helpful reply: first-timer planning for Tomorrowland Belgium 2026

**Festival:** Tomorrowland Belgium 2026 (比利时, Belgium)

For Tomorrowland Belgium 2026 (July 17–19 in Boom De Schorre), here are some practical first-timer tips from someone who’s done the festival circuit a few times:

**Getting there**
- Boom is about 30–40 minutes from Brussels by train. Trains are usually the easiest option during the festival — they run frequently and drop you close to the site.
- If you’re flying in, Brussels Airport (BRU) is the most convenient. Many travelers also use Brussels South (Charleroi), but it’s a bit farther out.

**Where to stay**
- Official Tomorrowland accommodation sells out fast. If that’s gone, look at hotels or hostels in Brussels or Antwerp and commute in by train.
- Book early — prices jump closer to July.

**Tickets & lineup**
- The lineup hasn’t been announced yet, so don’t stress about missing artists. Keep an eye on the official Tomorrowland website for updates.
- Only buy tickets through official channels to avoid scams.

**On-site tips**
- Bring a reusable water bottle — there are refill stations.
- Wear comfortable shoes; you’ll walk a lot between stages.
- The weather can be unpredictable, so pack a light rain jacket even if the forecast looks good.
- Charge your phone fully each night and consider a small power bank.

**General advice**
- Plan your group’s meeting points in case you get separated (phone signal can get spotty).
- Arrive early on day one to get familiar with the layout before it gets crowded.

If you have more specific questions (transport, packing, etc.), feel free to ask!

### Post 2

**Topic:** Helpful reply: travel + lodging logistics for Lost Lands 2026

**Content type:** guide

**Festival:** Lost Lands 2026 (美国, United States)

Lost Lands is held at Legend Valley in Ohio, and it’s a pretty remote site compared to city festivals, so planning your logistics early makes a huge difference.

**Getting there:**
Most people fly into Columbus (CMH), which is about an hour drive from Legend Valley. Rideshares can get pricey and unreliable in that area during the festival, so if you’re not driving, look into festival shuttles or group rides with campmates. Carpooling with people in your camp is usually the easiest and cheapest option.

**Lodging:**
Camping is the most common choice and keeps you close to the action. If you’re not into camping, hotels fill up fast in nearby towns like Thornville and Newark, so book as soon as you know your dates. Airbnbs in the surrounding area are also an option but tend to get snatched up quickly.

**First-timer tips:**
- Bring sturdy shoes; Legend Valley is dusty and the terrain can be rough.
- Pack for weather swings — September in Ohio can be warm during the day and chilly at night.
- Hydration is key; the site is big and you’ll be doing a lot of walking.
- If you’re camping, arrive early to get a good spot and give yourself time to set up before music starts.

If you want to coordinate with other attendees or find a camp group, communities like the Raven festival hub can be useful for connecting with people going solo or looking for crew.
