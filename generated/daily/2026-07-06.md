**Festival:** Tomorrowland Thailand 2026 (Pattaya, Thailand)

First off, congrats on planning for Tomorrowland’s Thailand debut! December 11–13, 2026 at Wisdom Valley in Pattaya is going to be huge.

Since the official timetable isn’t out yet, here’s some practical advice for first-timers:

**Travel & Accommodation**
- Pattaya is about a 1.5–2 hour drive from Bangkok’s Suvarnabhumi Airport. Most people either book a hotel in Pattaya proper or look for official Tomorrowland shuttles once they’re announced.
- Wisdom Valley is a dedicated event space, so check if the festival releases official accommodation packages — they usually do for Tomorrowland events.

**Weather & Packing**
- December is Thailand’s cool season, but "cool" still means ~25–30°C (77–86°F) during the day. Expect humidity and possible short rain showers.
- Pack light, breathable clothes, a poncho (not an umbrella — they block views), comfortable closed-toe shoes, and sunscreen.

**Lineup & Stages**
- You already have heavy hitters like Martin Garrix, Swedish House Mafia, Steve Aoki, Dimitri Vegas & Like Mike, Infected Mushroom, and Vini Vici confirmed.
- Genres range from EDM and House to Trance and Techno, so there’s something for everyone.
- Once the timetable drops, plan your must-see sets early — Tomorrowland stages are spread out and the production is massive, so walking between stages takes time.

**General Tips**
- Stay hydrated and pace yourself — three days in the Thai heat adds up.
- Bring a portable charger; you’ll be taking a ton of photos and videos.
- If you want to meet other ravers ahead of time, join Tomorrowland Thailand Facebook groups or subreddits — people often coordinate rides, hotels, and meetups there.

Keep an eye on https://thailand.tomorrowland.com for official updates on tickets, shuttles, and the full schedule. Have an amazing first Tomorrowland experience!

### Post 2

**Topic:** Helpful reply: travel + lodging logistics for Ultra Japan 2026

**Content type:** guide

**Festival:** Ultra Japan 2026 (Tokyo, Japan)

Odaiba is super easy to get to from most parts of Tokyo, so I’d recommend staying somewhere along the Yurikamome Line or even over in Shibuya/Shinjuku if you don’t mind a slightly longer train ride. The Yurikamome is a driverless elevated train and it’s the most direct way to get to the festival site—it also gives you great views of the bay on the way in.

If you’re flying in internationally, Narita and Haneda are both doable, but Haneda is much closer to Odaiba and will save you a lot of transit time. Keep in mind that Tokyo hotels can book up quickly around big events, so locking something down sooner rather than later is a good move.

Since the lineup and timetable aren’t out yet, I’d avoid buying anything non-refundable too far in advance. Once the schedule drops, you can plan which days you’ll actually want to attend. Also, Japanese festivals are very organized but can involve a fair amount of walking, so comfortable shoes are a must.

If you want to meet people ahead of time, there are usually Tokyo-based rave communities and group chats where folks coordinate meetups and share tips closer to the event.
