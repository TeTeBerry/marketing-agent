Hey! If you’re planning your first Tomorrowland Belgium trip for 2026 (July 17–19 at De Schorre in Boom), here are some practical tips from someone who’s been there before:

**Getting there:**
- Fly into Brussels Airport (BRU) — it’s the closest major airport. From there, you can take a train to Boom station, then shuttle buses or a short walk to the festival site.
- If you’re coming from nearby countries, trains and buses into Belgium are super reliable and often cheaper than flying.

**Where to stay:**
- Tomorrowland offers official accommodation packages (hotels, hostels, camping) that include shuttle transport — these are the easiest options for first-timers.
- If you want more flexibility, look for Airbnbs or hotels in Antwerp or Brussels and take the train + shuttle each day. Boom itself is small, so options there fill up fast.

**Tickets & lineup:**
- Tickets usually sell out extremely quickly, so keep an eye on the official Tomorrowland announcements for the exact sale dates.
- The 2026 lineup so far includes Dino, Luna & Lenthe (Trance), Lennert Wolfs (Future Bass, House, Moombahton), Flavour Drop, Alycia Bezgo b2b Portex (Techno, House, Trance), and 5NAPBACK b2b Karakals. The schedule is already published, so you can start planning which acts you want to see.

**First-timer tips:**
- Wear comfortable shoes — you’ll walk a lot between stages.
- Bring a reusable water bottle; there are refill stations everywhere.
- Download the official Tomorrowland app once it updates for 2026 to keep track of set times and any last-minute changes.
- If you want to meet people beforehand, join some of the fan communities or festival planning groups — lots of travelers coordinate there.

Let me know if you have more specific questions about transport, camping, or anything else!

### Post 2

**Topic:** Helpful reply: travel + lodging logistics for Lost Lands 2026

**Content type:** guide

**Festival:** Lost Lands 2026 (United States, United States)

Lost Lands 2026 is set for September 18–20 at Legend Valley in Ohio. Since the full lineup and schedule aren’t out yet, I’d focus on locking in lodging and transport first.

Legend Valley is pretty remote, so most people either camp on-site or stay in nearby towns like Thornville, Newark, or Zanesville. On-site camping sells out fast and is the easiest way to avoid long shuttle lines, but if you prefer a real bed, book a hotel soon—options fill up quickly once dates are confirmed.

For getting there, driving is the most common choice. If you’re flying, Columbus (CMH) is the closest major airport, about an hour away. Rideshares can be pricey and spotty in that area, so if you’re not driving, look into festival shuttles or connect with others in advance to split a rental car.

Pro tip: bring solid footwear and check the weather history for mid-September in Ohio—it can swing from hot days to chilly nights. If you’re camping, a small rug or mat makes a huge difference in keeping dust out of your tent.
