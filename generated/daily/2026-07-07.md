**Topic:** Helpful reply: first-timer planning for Tomorrowland Belgium 2026

**Content type:** guide

**Festival:** Tomorrowland Belgium 2026 (Boom, Belgium)

If this is your first Tomorrowland, a few things that really help with planning:

- **Dates & location:** It’s July 17–19, 2026 at De Schorre in Boom, Belgium. Brussels is the closest major airport, and there are usually solid train/bus options to Boom once schedules are finalized.
- **Accommodation:** Hotels near Boom sell out fast. A lot of people stay in Brussels or Antwerp and commute, or book official camping if that fits your vibe.
- **Lineup:** The full lineup isn’t out yet, so I wouldn’t lock in too many expectations. Keep an eye on announcements and plan your must-see sets once the schedule drops.
- **Tickets:** Only buy through official Tomorrowland channels when they go on sale. Resale scams are common, so avoid random third-party sites.
- **Pack smart:** De Schorre is mostly outdoor terrain, so comfortable shoes are a must. Weather in Belgium can shift quickly, so bring a light rain layer just in case.

If you want to keep track of lineup news and plan with friends, apps like Raven can be handy for organizing schedules once they’re released, but honestly a notes app and group chat work fine too.

### Post 2

**Topic:** Helpful reply: travel + lodging logistics for Lost Lands 2026

**Content type:** guide

**Festival:** Lost Lands 2026 (United States, United States)

Lost Lands 2026 is set for September 18–20 at Legend Valley in the US, so you’ll want to start planning logistics early since it’s a popular event and the area fills up fast.

For lodging, you’ve got a few solid options:
- **On-site camping**: This is the most convenient choice and the classic way to do Lost Lands. You’ll be right there for the music and won’t have to worry about daily shuttles or driving.
- **Nearby hotels**: There are limited hotel options within a reasonable drive, so book as soon as you know your dates. Check towns within 30–45 minutes of Legend Valley.
- **Airbnb / vacation rentals**: These can be a good middle ground if you want more comfort than camping but still want to stay close.

For travel:
- **Flying in**: The closest major airports are a bit of a drive away, so factor in rental car time or look for shared rides with other attendees.
- **Driving**: Legend Valley is rural, so having a car makes it easier to grab supplies, but parking passes sell out and traffic can be heavy on arrival and departure days.
- **Rideshare groups**: Join festival-specific Facebook groups or Reddit threads to coordinate carpools with other attendees.

General first-timer tips:
- Bring sturdy footwear — Legend Valley can get muddy if it rains.
- Pack layers; September weather can swing from warm days to chilly nights.
- If you’re camping, arrive early to get a good spot and avoid the longest entry lines.
- Hydrate and pace yourself across the three days.

Once the lineup and schedule are released, you can start mapping out which sets you want to prioritize. Until then, focus on securing your lodging and travel so you’re not scrambling later.
