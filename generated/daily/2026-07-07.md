**Topic:** Helpful reply: first-timer planning for Tomorrowland Belgium 2026

**Content type:** guide

**Festival:** Tomorrowland Belgium 2026 (Boom, Belgium)

For Tomorrowland Belgium 2026 (July 17–19 at De Schorre in Boom), here are some practical first-timer tips:

- **Accommodation:** Book as early as possible. Official DreamVille camping is the most convenient option and sells out fast, but there are also hotels and hostels in Boom, Antwerp, and Brussels if you don’t mind a short commute.
- **Transport:** The festival runs shuttles from nearby train stations and Brussels Airport. Trains to Boom are easy, and the local shuttle system is well organized once you’re in town.
- **Tickets:** Only buy through the official Tomorrowland shop or verified resale partners. Avoid third-party sites that look too good to be true.
- **Lineup:** The full schedule isn’t out yet, so keep an eye on the official site and app as the festival gets closer. In the meantime, brush up on past headliners and stages so you have a sense of the vibe.
- **Packing:** De Schorre is mostly open ground, so bring comfortable shoes, a rain jacket (Belgian weather can change quickly), and a refillable water bottle. Lockers and charging stations are available on-site if you want to travel light.
- **Crowds:** Main stage areas get very busy in the evenings. If you want a more relaxed experience, explore the smaller stages earlier in the day.

If you want to coordinate with other travelers or find a crew, communities like Raven can be useful for connecting with people going to the same event, but it’s totally optional.

### Post 2

**Topic:** Helpful reply: travel + lodging logistics for Lost Lands 2026

**Content type:** guide

**Festival:** Lost Lands 2026 (United States, United States)

For Lost Lands 2026 at Legend Valley (September 18–20), the most common setup is camping on-site since the venue is pretty remote in Ohio and there aren’t many hotels right next to it. If you’re driving, plan to arrive early each day because traffic getting into Legend Valley can back up badly, especially on Friday. Carpooling with your group helps a ton with parking logistics.

If camping isn’t your thing, your best bet is looking for hotels or Airbnbs in nearby towns like Thornville, Newark, or Zanesville and commuting in each day—just know the drive back after the headliners finish can take longer than expected. Bring layers too; September in Ohio can swing from hot during the day to pretty chilly at night once the sun goes down. As soon as the lineup and set times drop, map out your must-see acts so you’re not rushing between stages in the dark.
